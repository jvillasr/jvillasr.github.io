Why massive binaries?

Binaries set the final fates of many massive stars: they drive mass transfer, spin-up, and mergers, producing stripped stars, supernovae, X-ray binaries, and compact-object pairs.

By measuring binary demographics and interaction products across environments, we can connect what we observe today to the formation channels of compact companions and gravitational-wave sources.
